\section{Introduction}

This is motivated by our need to make an SSE QMC for the Rydberg hamiltonian.
\begin{align}
    H = & -\Omega \sum_{i = 1}^N \sigma^x_i - h \sum_{i=1}^n n_i - \sum_{\expval{i,j}} V_{ij} n_i n_j
\end{align}
Here, $n_i$ is simply an occupation number (i.e. either 0 or 1). We can therefore trivially map this to spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ with $n_i = \frac{1}{2}\left(\sigma_i^z + \mathbb{1} \right)$. The hamiltonian then becomes the following.
\begin{subequations}
    \begin{align}
        H = & - \Omega \sum_{i = 1}^N \sigma^x_i - \frac{h}{2} \sum_{i=1}^N \left(\sigma_i^z + \mathbb{1}\right) - \sum_{\expval{i,j}} V_{ij} \left(\sigma_i^z + \mathbb{1}\right) \left(\sigma_j^z + \mathbb{1}\right) \\
        =   & - \Omega \sum_{i = 1}^N \sigma^x_i - \frac{h}{2} \sum_{i=1}^N \left(\sigma_i^z + \mathbb{1}\right) - \sum_{\expval{i,j}} V_{ij} \left(\sigma_i^z \sigma_j^z + \sigma_i^z + \sigma_j^z + \mathbb{1}\right)
    \end{align}
\end{subequations}
This is extremely similar to a LTFIM (general $J_{ij}$ for all pairs, assuming $J_{ij} > 0 \quad \forall \quad i,j$ as in the Rydberg case since $V_{ij} = \frac{C}{R_{ij}^6}$).
\begin{align}
    H = & -\Omega \sum_{i = 1}^N \sigma^x_i - h \sum_{i=1}^N \sigma_i^z + \sum_{\expval{i,j}} J_{ij} \sigma_i^z \sigma_j^z \qquad \mathrm{LTFIM}
\end{align}
Thus, if an efficient SSE formalism exists for such a Hamiltonian, then it is foreseeable that one may exist for the Rydberg Hamiltonian. So, let's work with the LTFIM as a toy model for the Rydberg Hamiltonian.

\section{Longitudinal Field TFIM (LTFIM)}

In the SSE formalism, we must decompose the Hamiltonian into diagonal or off-diagonal site or bond operators. Let's adopt the following Hamiltonian decomposition and disregard general $J_{ij}$ interactions for now.

\subsection{Justification for LTFIM Hamiltonian decomposition} \label{sec:H_decomp}

Originally, we thought it was a good idea to decompose our Hamiltonian this way for the LTFIM.
\begin{subequations}
    \begin{align}
        H_{0,0}  = & 1                           \\
        H_{-1,a} = & h \sigma_i^x                \\
        H_{0,a}  = & \Omega(\sigma_i^z + 2) + h  \\
        H_{1,a}  = & J(\sigma_i^z\sigma_j^z + 1)
    \end{align}
\end{subequations}
This would give us the following matrix elements.
\begin{subequations}
    \begin{align}
        \matrixel{\uparrow}{H_{-1,a}}{\downarrow} =              & \matrixel{\downarrow}{H_{-1,a}}{\uparrow} = h                             \\
        \matrixel{\uparrow}{H_{0,a}}{\uparrow} =                 & 3\Omega + h\qquad \matrixel{\downarrow}{H_{0,a}}{\downarrow} = h + \Omega \\
        \matrixel{\uparrow\uparrow}{H_{1,a}}{\uparrow\uparrow} = & \matrixel{\downarrow\downarrow}{H_{1,a}}{\downarrow\downarrow} = 2J
    \end{align}
\end{subequations}
We hoped that this decomposition would be able to replicate TFIM in the $\Omega \rightarrow 0$ limit. Indeed, it turns out that the diagonal-updates are trivially equivalent if $\Omega = 0$. However, consider the following scheme for the off-diagonal directed loop updates.

\begin{itemize}
    \item Loops are formed freely (i.e. pass through every vertex) except when $H_{0,a}$ or $H_{-1,a}$ are encountered.
    \item For example, if $H_{0,a}$ is encountered at a spin-up site, we {\it pass-through} with probability
        \begin{subequations}
            \begin{align}
                P_{pass} = \frac{h + \Omega}{h + \Omega + h} \\
                \intertext{or we {\it stop} (i.e. cluster terminates) with probability}
                P_{stop} = \frac{h}{h + \Omega + h}
            \end{align}
        \end{subequations}
\end{itemize}
These probabilities are heat-bath probabilities and are related to the two options we can execute on when ecountering $H_{0,a}$ in loop formation (i.e. $\matrixel{\uparrow}{H_{0,a}}{\uparrow} \rightarrow \matrixel{\downarrow}{H_{0,a}}{\downarrow} = h + \Omega$ for passing through, or $\matrixel{\uparrow}{H_{0,a}}{\uparrow} \rightarrow \matrixel{\downarrow}{H_{-1,a}}{\uparrow} = h$ for stopping).

In TFIM, our loop formation is deterministic, given the configuration of the simulation cell; we halt loops at site operators. Thus, in the LTFIM formalism above, if $\Omega = 0$ we hope that $P_{pass} = 0$ and $P_{stop} = 1$. However, this is clearly not the case as one can see. Here, we get $P_{pass} = P_{stop} = \frac{1}{2}$.

Consider a different Hamiltonian decomposition.
\begin{subequations}
    \begin{align}
        H_{0,0}  = & 1                           \\
        H_{-1,a} = & h \sigma_i^x                \\
        H_{0,a}  = & h                           \\
        H_{1,a}  = & \Omega(\sigma_i^z + 2)      \\
        H_{2,a}  = & J(\sigma_i^z\sigma_j^z + 1)
    \end{align}
\end{subequations}
This would give us the following matrix elements.
\begin{subequations}
    \begin{align}
        \matrixel{\uparrow}{H_{-1,a}}{\downarrow} =              & \matrixel{\downarrow}{H_{-1,a}}{\uparrow} = h                       \\
        \matrixel{\uparrow}{H_{0,a}}{\uparrow} =                 & \matrixel{\downarrow}{H_{0,a}}{\downarrow} = h                      \\
        \matrixel{\uparrow}{H_{1,a}}{\uparrow} =                 & 3\Omega \qquad \matrixel{\downarrow}{H_{1,a}}{\downarrow} = \Omega  \\
        \matrixel{\uparrow\uparrow}{H_{2,a}}{\uparrow\uparrow} = & \matrixel{\downarrow\downarrow}{H_{2,a}}{\downarrow\downarrow} = 2J
    \end{align}
\end{subequations}
If we follow the same rules for directed loops as mentioned before when we encounter $H_{1,a}$, we are guaranteed to reproduce TFIM in the $\Omega = 0$ limit. If we encounter $H_{1,a}$ with a spin-up configuration, we {\it pass-through} with probability
\begin{subequations}
    \begin{align}
        P_{pass} = \frac{\Omega}{\Omega + h} \\
        \intertext{while we {\it stop} with probability
        }    
        P_{stop} = \frac{h}{\Omega + h}.
    \end{align}
\end{subequations}
These probabilities are once again heat-bath probabilities and are related to the two options we can execute on when ecountering $H_{1,a}$ in loop formation (i.e. $\matrixel{\uparrow}{H_{1,a}}{\uparrow} \rightarrow \matrixel{\downarrow}{H_{1,a}}{\downarrow} = \Omega$ for passing through, or $\matrixel{\uparrow}{H_{1,a}}{\uparrow} \rightarrow \matrixel{\downarrow}{H_{-1,a}}{\uparrow} = h$ for stopping).
The advantages with this decomposition are clear.

We've added a constant shift to the LTFIM Hamiltonian of $N\Omega + 2Nh + 2N_bJ$. 

\subsection{Diagonal Updates (Finite $T$)}

The diagonal update proceeds similarily to that for the regular TFIM. Traverse the list of all $M$ operators in the propagation sequence, ignore off-diagonal operators (still only the $H_{-1,a}$ operator), and perform this MH procedure.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item If a diagonal operator is encountered ($H_{0,a}, H_{1,a}$, $H_{2,a}$), remove it with probability
          \begin{align}
              P = & \mathrm{min}\left(\frac{M-n+1}{\beta\left[2N\Omega + 4Nh + 4N_b J\right]},1\right)
          \end{align}
        (The value of the denominator comes from summing all diagonal matrix elements)
    \item If the null operator, $H_{0,0}$, is encountered, insert a diagonal operator with the following procedure.
          \begin{itemize}
              \item Decide whether or not to insert a diagonal operator with the probability
                    \begin{align}
                        P = \mathrm{min}\left(\frac{\beta\left[2N\Omega + 4Nh + 4N_b J\right]}{M-n},1\right).
                    \end{align}
              \item If it was decided to insert a diagonal operator, choose the operator to insert with probabilities
                    \begin{align}
                        P_{H_{0,a}} = & \frac{2N\Omega}{2N\Omega + 4Nh + 4N_b J}, \\
                        P_{H_{1,a}} = & \frac{4Nh}{2N\Omega + 4Nh + 4N_bJ}                           \\
                        P_{H_{2,a}} = & \frac{4\sum_{\expval{i,j}} J}{2N\Omega + 4Nh + 4N_b J}.
                    \end{align}
              \item If it was decided to insert $H_{0,a}$, then choose a random site (probability $1 / N$) to put the operator. Note that it can be put at any site regardless of spin! \\
              
                    If it was decided to insert $H_{1,a}$, then choose to insert it at a site with $\uparrow$ with probability $3N_{\uparrow}h / (N_{\downarrow}h + 3N_{\uparrow}h)$ or at a site with $\downarrow$ probability $N_{\downarrow}h / (N_{\downarrow}h + 3N_{\uparrow}h)$. \\

                    If it was decided to insert $H_{2,a}$, then choose a random bond $(k,l)$ to put the operator. If the chosen bond has a local spin configuration that prevents the insertion (i.e. spins are antiparallel), then do not insert the bond operator and the entire move has failed.
          \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Off-Diagonal Updates}

As we explained in Sec.~\ref{sec:H_decomp}, the decomposition of the Hamiltonian was explicity chosen in order for the diagonal and off-diagonal updates to replicate the TFIM SSE formalism in the limit of zero transverse field ($\Omega \rightarrow 0$). The off-diagonal updates follow the directed-loops procedure wherein isolated loops in the simulation cell are formed and spins that ``touch'' the loops are flipped (some operators are also correspondingly flipped).
\begin{itemize}
    \item A loop passes through all legs of the $H_{2,a}$ operator (i.e. it stems out of the 4 legs).
    \item Loops must terminate upon entering a leg of $H_{-1,a}$ or $H_{0,a}$. 
    \item Loops that encounter $H_{1,a}$ must decide to terminate there or pass through probabilistically.
    \item Loops are flipped with probability 1/2.
\end{itemize}
